Dental Implant Procedure

A dental implant is a metal screw used in place of the root of a missing tooth. The screw is usually made of titanium and is attached to the bone in your jaw. Your dental professional will attach an artificial tooth to the implant. Multiple dental appointments are needed to prepare and fit your dental implant.

What is a dental implant?

The dental implant is attached to the bone where the roots of the missing tooth sat. It can support a crown, dental bridge, or dentures. A crown is an artificial tooth made to match your other teeth. A dental bridge is multiple crowns attached together. Dentures replace all teeth on the upper and/or lower jaw.

Dental implant procedure

The dental implant procedure is the operation used to replace missing teeth, usually done in stages over a few months. First, x-rays are taken to see your jaw clearly. The implant is placed in the jawbone under local anaesthetic. Over 3 months, the bone grows around the implant, holding it in place. Meanwhile, your crown, bridge, or denture is made in a laboratory and attached once healing is complete.

Benefits of a dental implant

  • Improved confidence in how your teeth look
  • Ability to eat normally
  • Feels like your natural teeth
  • Procedure does not involve other teeth

Preparing for a dental implant

Discuss the benefits and risks with your dental professional. Check that you have enough bone, healthy gums, and that any medical conditions or medications are suitable. Some factors that may affect suitability include age over 60, osteoporosis, cancer, bleeding disorders, gum disease, diabetes, or smoking.

Recovery

Bone growth around the implant takes approximately three months. After the procedure, you may experience bruising, pain, swelling of gums or face, or minor bleeding. Follow your dental professional’s instructions for care, which may include using mouthwash instead of brushing, eating soft foods, and taking medications if necessary.

Possible complications

  • Infection
  • Nerve damage
  • Sinus problems
  • Injury or damage to surrounding teeth

Aftercare

  • Brush teeth and gums twice a day
  • Clean between implant and other teeth using floss, interdental brushes, or water flosser
  • Have regular dental check-ups
  • If swelling or inflammation occurs, have it checked

Alternatives to dental implants

  • Removable dentures
  • Dental bridge attached to surrounding teeth

Costs

Dental implants can be expensive. Check all costs with your dental professional and see if your insurance, Veteran’s Card, or Medicare covers part of it.
