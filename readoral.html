Treatment Details: How Can I Improve My Oral Hygiene?

Excellent oral hygiene protects your teeth and gums and keeps your smile beautiful. Here are some general oral hygiene instructions to keep your smile healthy:

Daily Oral Hygiene Steps

  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day: Use fluoride toothpaste and a soft-bristled toothbrush. Medium or hard bristles can damage gums and enamel. Place the toothbrush at a 45-degree angle toward your gums to sweep away plaque and bacteria. Brush all tooth surfaces, including backs and sides.
  • Floss once daily: Brushing alone cannot reach between teeth. Use about 18 inches of floss, wrap around your fingers, guide it in a C shape around each tooth, and move up and down 10 strokes per tooth. Interproximal brushes and dental picks can help if needed. Water flossers can remove debris but do not replace traditional floss.
  • Brush your tongue: Your tongue holds bacteria. Brush it with your toothbrush or use a tongue scraper.
  • Use antibacterial mouthwash: Helps reduce bacteria and plaque. Choose alcohol-free formulas to prevent dry mouth.
  • Visit your dentist regularly: Routine exams and cleanings are essential. Many people do well with six-month visits, but those prone to cavities, gum disease, or other issues may need more frequent appointments.
  • Avoid smoking and tobacco products: Smoking is a leading cause of gum disease and oral cancer. Quitting helps maintain oral health.
  • Remember, the best oral hygiene routine is one that you can practice consistently. Talk to your dentist about a personalized oral health regimen to meet your needs.

Which Oral Hygiene Products Should I Use?

Look for the ADA Seal of Acceptance on oral health products. This seal indicates the product has been scientifically tested and approved. Your dentist may also give personalized product recommendations based on your specific needs.

Advantages of Good Oral Hygiene

  • Healthier teeth and gums
  • A beautiful smile
  • Fresher breath
  • Reduced need for dental work such as fillings, crowns, bridges, implants, or dentures
  • Lowered risk of heart disease, stroke, diabetes, and other health concerns
  • Reduced risk of oral cancer
  • Preventive dental care is more affordable than restorative or emergency dental care
